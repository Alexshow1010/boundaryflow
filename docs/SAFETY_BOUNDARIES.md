# BoundaryFlow Safety Boundaries

BoundaryFlow v0.1 is a simulator of a future safety-system concept. These boundaries define what the simulator does not do and what any future real-world implementation would need to preserve.

## No Reverse Location Exposure

The restricted-person surface must never reveal the protected person’s precise location, residence, movement direction, or navigation route. Boundary feedback communicates proximity and required separation, not where to find someone.

## No Public Tracking

BoundaryFlow is not a public people-tracking system. Case and device information must not be exposed through public search, feeds, maps, or directories.

## No Vigilante Use

The product must not enable retaliation, stalking, harassment, public shaming, or private enforcement. Boundary and event information is limited to authorized safety purposes.

## No Private Self-Created Monitoring Targets

Individuals must not be able to create private monitoring targets or attach monitoring rules to another person without valid judicial or authorized basis in the intended future system.

## No Automated Guilt Determination

Distance, device, persistence, tamper, or boundary events are signals for authorized review. They do not establish intent, guilt, criminal liability, or legal culpability.

## No Predictive Policing

BoundaryFlow does not predict who will commit an offense, generate risk scores about people, or automate policing decisions.

## No Real Emergency-Service Simulation Claims

Silent Notify, One-Tap Help, notification relay, escalation, and audio states are interface simulations. They do not contact emergency services, police, courts, safety contacts, or monitoring centers.

## Judicial or Authorized Activation Only

In the intended future product model, activation and protective-zone configuration require appropriate judicial or otherwise authorized processes. The v0.1 simulator does not implement or validate those processes.

## Simulated Data Only

All people, cases, devices, distances, events, notifications, evidence records, compliance states, battery values, and synchronization times in v0.1 are local mock data.

## No Precise Protected Location

The interface uses a protected core and relative distance. It displays no real address, residence, coordinate, route, or map position.

## No Real Evidence Admissibility Claims

Timeline and evidence records are simulated UI states. There is no production chain of custody, cryptographic attestation, evidentiary validation, or claim of legal admissibility.

## Required Human Review

Human legal, security, privacy, accessibility, safety, hardware, reliability, and operational review is required before any real-world implementation or pilot. A future system would also require formal threat modeling, abuse-case testing, data-governance rules, authorization controls, failure-mode analysis, incident-response planning, and jurisdiction-specific legal review.

## System Decision Boundary

The simulator responds to distance, direction, persistence, device state, and boundary conditions. It does not judge people or determine criminal liability.

BoundaryFlow is not a substitute for emergency services, legal advice, or an operational personal-safety system.
